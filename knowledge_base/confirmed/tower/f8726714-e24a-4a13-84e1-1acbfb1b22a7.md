# The Eiffel Tower is a wrought-iron lattice tower.

**Verdict**: `CONFIRMED`  **Confidence**: 0.85  **Propagated**: 0.85  **Consensus**: 0.00

**Type**: historical  |  **Volatility**: 0.05  |  **Status**: current

## Judge reasoning

The claim survives with adjusted confidence. Independent verification confirms the Eiffel Tower is constructed of puddled iron (fer forgé in French) in a lattice structure. Britannica and multiple engineering sources confirm this. The challenger's lowering of confidence to 0.65 was excessive but not unfounded - they correctly noted that most accessible sources are secondary (Wikipedia, educational sites) rather than primary 1889 construction contracts. However, this is a verification gap, not evidence of falsification. The puddled iron vs. modern wrought iron distinction is a real metallurgical difference (puddled iron has higher phosphorus content and different production method), but both are forms of wrought iron family. The claim 'wrought-iron lattice tower' is substantially true. The challenger failed to falsify - they could only note source quality limitations, not contradictory evidence.

## What would change this verdict

Would require credible evidence that the Eiffel Tower's primary structural material was cast iron, steel, or another material distinct from wrought iron/puddled iron. The challenger's points about primary source access are valid but do not constitute falsification.
